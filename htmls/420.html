Disaster Recovery Work Continues, Wet Weather on the Way

The State of Texas, the City of Austin and local counties have issued official disaster declarations for our area due to the extent of the devastation left in the wake of Winter Storm Mara. The new challenge of forecasted thunderstorms and high winds on Tuesday and Wednesday could further complicate ongoing restoration and recovery efforts.

Safety is Key in the Days Ahead

Trees may still have lingering damage from the ice, and branches can still fall. Be careful walking under trees. Avoid parking near or under branches that might give way and damage your car or cause personal injury. Avoid leaving children and pets unsupervised near trees as this next round of weather comes through our area. Watch for lightning and severe weather warnings.

Austin Energy crews work in all weather conditions 24/7 and will continue their work to bring power back on to the remaining 9,000 customers who need electricity restored. Please remember to slow down when you drive near a crew working so they can complete their work as safely as possible, especially as conditions worsen.

Emergency Support Available

State Assistance
The State of Texas disaster declaration means assistance will become available to people and communities with property damage. Report damage through the Individual State of Texas Assessment Tool Damage Survey and include as much detail as possible. 

Shelter
For individuals and families without electricity who are unable to secure alternative accommodations, call 3-1-1 or 512-974-2000 to request overnight lodging. The City of Austin emergency shelter has sleeping cots, shower facilities, food/water, pet sheltering and charging stations. After requesting, expect a call from City staff between 7 a.m. and 7 p.m. with information on next steps. The emergency shelter will remain open until all power is restored in the Austin Energy service area.
