Working with coordinate systems is easier than ever!
Projections and coordinate systems are one of the more confusing parts of working with geospatial data - and we're here to help! We've made it easy to get your data in the projection you need so it shows up in the right spot, every time. 

Read on for three ways that we've made working with projections a piece of cake üç∞!

1) Find your NAD83 zone in the download menu
The NAD83 datum is one of the most popular. We've added the NAD83 state planes right in the download menu so you can see exactly where your data is and what state plane projection you need.

In the Download menu, turn on the "NAD83 Zones Overlay" toggle, and see all of the zones in the US. Click on the zone in the map to see the name of your zone, as well as the exact EPSG code in the attribute table!
Visit Equator

2) Understand your data's original coordinate system
By default, your download will be in the projection that the LiDAR was initially collected in. We've made the download panel clearer to show you exactly what that source projection is. 
Visit Equator

3) Search for the projection you need by name 

Did you know that you can search for your projection right in the download tab? When you download your data, you can search for the projection you need with text or find it with the EPSG code.
